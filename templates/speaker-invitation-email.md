# Speaker Invitation Email — Template

A reusable template for inviting speakers, panellists and partner teams to deliver a
session for the **X-CITED Digital Community of Practice** (GW4 digital RTP network).

It is derived from a batch of real invitations and keeps the parts that stayed constant
while pulling the parts that changed into `{{placeholders}}` and **optional blocks**.

---

## How to use

1. Fill every `{{placeholder}}` (see the table below).
2. Keep or delete each `[OPTIONAL: …]` block — guidance for when to include it is inline.
3. Delete this `## How to use` section and the placeholder table before sending.
4. Aim for ~200–250 words. The reader is busy: explain *why them*, not the logistics.

### Placeholders

| Placeholder        | What it is                          | Examples |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------|----------|
| `{{GREETING}}`     | Salutation, matched to formality    | `Dear Simon,` · `Dear Professor Dencik,` · `Dear colleagues,` |
| `{{TOPIC}}`        | The session theme in a few words    | high-performance computing and AI infrastructure · FAIR data and reproducibility |
| `{{WHY_THEM}}`     | 1–2 sentences linking *their* work to the topic — the heart of the email | "Given your work leading Coding for Reproducible Research at Exeter…" |
| `{{THE_ASK}}`      | What you'd like them to share        | "share your perspective on the development of Isambard-AI and the opportunities it creates for research" |
| `{{AUDIENCE_NOTE}}`| One line on who'll be in the room    | "Research Software Engineers, data scientists, research technologists and others who shape how research is supported" |
| `{{NAME}}`         | Your name                            | |
| `{{ROLE}}`         | Your role                            | |

## Template

{{GREETING}}

I'm writing on behalf of the X-CITED Digital Community of Practice, a network of digital
Research Technical Professionals (RTPs) from across the GW4 universities.
[OPTIONAL — expanded membership; include for speakers outside the RTP world who need the context:
Our members include Research Software Engineers, data scientists, bioinformaticians,
digital humanists and research IT specialists supporting research across a wide range of
disciplines.]

We're planning a session on {{TOPIC}}, and {{WHY_THEM}}.

We'd be delighted if you would join us to {{THE_ASK}}.

The session would be online and typically runs for around 30–45 minutes, followed by
discussion and Q&A. The audience would be {{AUDIENCE_NOTE}}, so there is usually plenty of
engaging discussion.
[OPTIONAL — format flexibility; include when a workshop/panel could suit the topic:
We'd also be very happy to explore a workshop-style format if that better suits the topic.]
[OPTIONAL — time zone; include for speakers outside the UK:
As our audience is UK-based, we'd be very happy to work around time zones to find a
convenient slot.]

If this is something you'd be interested in, we'd love to discuss possible dates.
[OPTIONAL — suggest a colleague; include when writing to a team/org, or a contact who may
not be the right speaker:
If another member of the team would be better placed to speak, we'd also be grateful for
any suggestions.]

Best wishes,

{{NAME}}
{{ROLE}}
X-CITED Digital Community of Practice

## Filled example

> Dear Eilis,
>
> I'm writing on behalf of the X-CITED Digital Community of Practice, a network of digital
> Research Technical Professionals (RTPs) from across the GW4 universities.
>
> We're planning a session on FAIR data and reproducibility, and given your work leading
> Coding for Reproducible Research at Exeter and your involvement with the Software
> Sustainability Institute, we thought you would be an ideal person to speak to this
> challenge.
>
> We'd be delighted if you would join us to share practical approaches, lessons learned and
> examples that RTPs can take back to the projects and researchers they support.
>
> The session would be online and typically runs for around 30–45 minutes, followed by
> discussion and Q&A. The audience would be Research Software Engineers, data scientists and
> research technologists from across GW4, so there is usually plenty of engaging discussion.
> We'd also be very happy to explore a workshop-style format if that better suits the topic.
>
> If this is something you'd be interested in, we'd love to discuss possible dates.
>
> Best wishes,
>
> [Name]
> [Role]
> X-CITED Digital Community of Practice
